About Us

The 1842 Scholars Program offers students the opportunity to engage with like-minded cadets through professional development, mentorship, study abroad porgrams, and more. Scholarship awards are $2000 for in-state students and $6000 for out-of-state students. The goal of the program is to identity highly motivated students, immerse them in a cohort community where they can explore their academic passions, provide them with opportunites to take intellectual risks, and develop them as thinkers and leaders. The organization began in 2021, with the Class of 2025 becoming the first 1842 Scholars.


How To Apply

Incoming freshmen cadets are automatically considered for the 1842 Scholarship Program, based on standard application for admission and provided high school credentials. Candidates are identified by a wholistic review of their high school credentials and extracurricular activities. Candidates are contacted by the Office of Financial Aid & Scholarships and invited to apply to the 1842 Honor Program. Current recipients must meet eligibilty standards yearly to be considered for renewal. This often means meeting a GPA requirement.


Leadership Council

As a young organization, the Leadership Council helps to direct the 1842 Scholarship Program inorder to create a legacy of excellence. The mission of the Leadership Council is to "Ensure the Contiunal Education & Recognition of Student/Cadets Who Excel In Academica." The Leadership Council is lead by the President, and is made up of the Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer. Individuals not in a leadership position are also freely available to attend Leadership Council Meetings. The Leadership Council is also responsible for the creation, maintaince, and enaction of the 1842 Articles of Organization.
